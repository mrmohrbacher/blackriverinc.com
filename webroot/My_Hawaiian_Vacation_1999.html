My Hawai'ian Vacation 1999

Did you know that the Hawaiian alphabet has only 12 letters; every syllable ends in vowel and the ' mark means a unvoiced glottal stop (like the - in oh-oh!)? Yeah, so what anyway! I'm back from across the ocean. Before I left I was a neurotic depressive. Now, I'm a neurotic, depressive with a tan...and less hair. I shaved off my beard and got my annual haircut.

We stayed on the west (leeward) side of the island in the Kona district, just south of the town of Kailua-Kona in a condo on Ali'i (A-LEE<>EE) Drive right on the shore. On the windward side of the island it rains... a lot. On the leeward side it never rains. The joke is that the weather map for the Kona coast is painted on the wall of the television station: low 65- high 85 slight chance of showers up-slope. It's correct 360 out of 365 days of the year.

I snorkeled in the lagoons a couple of time with my kids. Rented a bike. Walked across the caldera of a volcano. Read 3 1/2 science fiction novels. Ate a lot of fish, mostly ahi (tuna), some mackerel and mahi'mahi. But no humuhumunukanukaapua'a (it's the state fish) The only sushi I had was tuna fish salad maki from a vendor at the airport in Honolulu (ho-NO-loo-loo). Frankly, the food is better in Chicago than in Hawai'i.

Yes, guys, the beaches are populated with wahini in skimpy beachwear...who are all old enough to get the senior citizens discount at Denny's. Snorkeling in clear tropical waters is way big fun. A soon as you put your mask in the water, you are in the middle of aquarium, except the Yellow Tang are 4X larger and they are swimming all around you, along with Puffer Fish, Parrot Fish, Trigger Fish, etc. mostly snacking on the coral at the bottom of the lagoon.When Ben saw this for the first time he made a quick 180 for the shore, but came back to hang onto me as we circled around in the water. The water was cool, but not cold. Mister Benjamin "0% Body-Fat" Mohrbacher only lasted about 15 minutes before I could feel him shivering against me like a telegraph key and we had to come back in for good. Peter and I did several circuits around the lagoon and saw many shamelessly gaudy fish. He discovered that the layer of water over your back is NOT a sun-screen.

Riding a bicycle will definitely either get you shape or kill you. It was a sixty minute ride up to the intersection of King Kamehameha III Drive and the beltway (alt. 500 ft) from the condo (alt. 5 ft.), and about about a five minute ride down.

The island of Hawai'i is basically two big shield volcanoes, Mauna Loa, and Mauna Kea. We took a peek through the 25 cent binoculars at a resort in the middle of a volcanic coastal desert in the South Kohala district and could see the snow on the peak of Mauna Kea (alt. 13,795 ft)

Pele, the fiery goddess of lava lives inside the volcanoes, and occasionally rearranges the furniture. When she does this, one of the Mauna will spit up another million cubic yards of 2000 degree molten basalt through a vent, burying whatever is in its path to the sea. The lava cools into either A'A - crumbly looking lava,or PA'HOEHOE - smooth looking lava depending upon the amount of dissolved gases and original temperature, and voila!, more land! Will Rogers was wrong, they are making more of it. A'A flows look like a badly managed construction sight, where they tore the ground up and then forgot to do anything else. PA'HOEHOE looks like a dragon took a dump over several square miles. This pretty much covers the geology of the Hawai'i.

My brother-in-law took myself, and the boys, Peter and Ben, for a hike across the Kilauea caldera from the edge of the Halema'uma'u caldera (which is a "little" crater only about a 1 mile across and 200 ft deep) for a 2 mile hike over terrain that could be used for a great battling-aliens-in-space movie, except the workman's comp payments for torn ankle ligaments, shattered knees and broken legs would drive you into bankruptcy. It was all PA'HOEHOE with man deep cracks in it and steaming vents stinking of sulphur - more than a little like Hell's parking lot. The "trail" consisted of 4" dots painted onto the rock every 50 yards and sacred rock cairns. The sacred rock cairns were about 3 ft high and made out of broken pieces of PA'HOEHOE, so their visibility factor was not high. The climb out of the Kilauea caldera is up a 200 ft slope made of pumice. The 30 minute hike took us a little under 2 hours. Nancy was supposed to meet us at the trail head with the car. I think she was not pleased when 90 minutes had passed and 2/3 of her offspring were last seen walking into a smoking volcano. She started orbiting the rim of the crater, checking for our presence at the other trail heads. Which means when we emerged at the end of "Desolation Trail", she was nowhere in sight. Eventually she made it back to our site while we sat remarking on the interesting way the light, misting rain was being driven sideways across the landscape at 20 mph. After a few remarks were exchanged regarding the original time estimates vs. the actual time past, we drove off down slope.

All of the "big island" has a resident population of around 140,000 spread over maybe a several dozen towns. The biggest town is Hilo on the east (windward) side. We did a circumlocution of Hawai'i (the island, not the state) by car (although there are several INTERstate highways on the island of Oahu.). Its was about 2 1/2 hours counter-clockwise from Kona to Volcano National Park. Another 2 1/2 hours from the park around the north-east and back around into Kona. And that's the BIG island. By the look of the housing, most permanent residents are lower working class types living in corrugated sheet metal and concrete block houses. But a lot of them have great views down slope to the sea, or into some lush, tropical valley (on the windward side).

We made a side-trip to Oahu. My choice was to go to the USS Arizona memorial. It's the favorite stop for Japanese tourists in the whole state. Over a thousand BOYS died in less than nine minutes when an Japanese armor-piercing bomb exploded in the forward magazine. (Movies always show MEN as soldiers. On the way home in San Francisco, a platoon of GI's were in the airport lounge waiting for our flight. I don't think they looked old enough to drive, let alone handle guns!)

Nancy and Ben got to notch off another state capital (Honolulu), the first that was not a neo-classical columns and rotunda affair. The Hawi'ian capitol building holds both houses of the legislature and the executive on four floors. There is a central courtyard open to the sky, designed so that if the people had something to say enmasse, they were certain to be heard.

Peter began his quest to play in every Ultrazone in the country by doing rounds in Waikiki and Pearl City.

Want to own a house in paradise? Upslope, maybe 120K$-180K$, depending on the view (see earlier description of housing stock). On the beach side of Ali'i Drive your looking at 300K$ for a three-bedroom, two-bath condo. The condo we stayed at was 4-bedroom, 3 full bath, one bedroom was a master suite, witha lanai (that's Hawai'ian for a nice balcony) and one parking spot would probably go for a cool 1/2 a mil. Location, location, location! You can probably get a nice studio condo on the upslope side of Ali'i for only 200K$. Actually. development is not a sure fire thing. We saw several Hot!Hot!Hot! housing and shopping mall sites that consisted of graded lava. Since they were generally A'A, it was hard to tell a failed housing development from a normal lava flow.

I hoped to have some snaps, but I discovered that the back of the camera had popped open somewhere climbing out of Kilauea, so we will see what images survived, if any. Check my website in a couple of weeks and see if I figure out how to get some good color scans onto the pages.

Anyway, A-lo-ha (hello, good-bye, whatever) from Chicago.

mike

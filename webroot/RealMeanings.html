When working with software developers, it's very important to know their "lingo". By understanding these key phrases when they occur in conversations, reports, and documentation, you too will be able to plumb the subtle and deep reasoning of a software developers mind.
 
Key Software Development Phrases
Analysts
"That's very interesting" "Why didn't you just kill the Accounts Receivable clerk years ago and save us all from this torment."
"We're deep into the information gathering phase." "We're trying to make sense of the end result of decades of irrational decisions made by buffoons who couldn't run a lemonade stand."
"This specification is meant to be a 'living document'" "We couldn't figure it out either. We were hoping you would."
"You could look that up." "...before you bother me...again."
"I'll have to get back to you on that." "Go away, and never come near me again."
Designers
"That's very interesting" "That's the stupidest thing I've ever heard in my life."
"What is the purpose of this feature?" "Were you designing, or on designer drugs when you thought of it."
"The purpose of the design phase is really 'risk management'." 1) "Yeah. There was a risk our managers might find out we don't know what we're talking about."
2) "Yeah. There was a risk we might actually finish the project before the end of the year and would have to manage to find new contracts."
"I'll have to get back to you on that." "I haven't had my coffee, yet. Please, go away until I am able to grow premier arabica beans in my backyard and have them ground between hand-made limestone tables."
Coders
"That's very interesting" "Go teach your grandmother to suck eggs."
"This code is very simple" "...so even you shouln't have any trouble figuring it out."
"The code is written, but not debugged" "I'm about 1/4 done with the project."
"We could not reproduce the error" "We know our code works. It's not our fault QA can't figure out how to run it."
"I'll have to get back to you on that." "After I've actually read the spec."
Project Managers
"That's very interesting" "Whose pants leg are you trying to blow smoke up, anyway."
"We are reviewing the feature set on that one." "Not in this release, bucko."
"We are making steady progress." "...backwards."

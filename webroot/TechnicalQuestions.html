Preface

The key issue facing you, as a technical manager, or as a team leader, is having good-quality people at hand. There are several dimensions to people-quality: initiative, reliability, honesty, ability to play well with others, hygiene, etc., all of which are very important, but when looking for an engineer or technician to add to your staff, there is the very critical "K-T-S." factor to consider.

The K-T-S factor is harder to quantify than it might seem. One approach taken by many organizations is to apply a standardized test to applicants, such BrainBench. Another approach is to look for certifications. The one most commonly used by headhunters is to apply a first-order sieve to the stack of resumes - the "bingo card" method (if your resume contains all of the same buzzwords as the job requisition: "BINGO!, You may be a winner!)

Each of these approaches has their utility, and each has its problems. A standardized test actually tests for the ability to take standardized tests as much as it tests for mastery of subject matter. A long list of certifications leaves you wondering when the applicant had time to do his job in between taking all of those certification tests.

SQL

Describe the ACID rule for Transactions

Atomic
All operations carried out within the scope of the transaction are as one; either they all happen, or none happen.
Consistent
The result of a transaction is the database in a state consistent with it's functional requirements.
Isolated
The operations of a transaction are not affected by other transaction happening at the same time. This will allow the transaction to be repeatable.
Durable
Once a transaction is commited, it stays committed. No failure can undo a committed transaction.

Describe the difference between CLUSTERED and UNCLUSTERED indices.

Indices are implemented as a type of tree, with pointers in the index referring to pages within the database store itself.

The leaf nodes of a CLUSTERED index are the data pages themselves.

The leaf nodes of a NONCLUSTERED index are pointers to the data pages.

Follow up to previous: How many CLUSTERED indices are allowed on a table?

One. If there were more than one, that would imply that a table could have more than one primary ordering of its data pages.
